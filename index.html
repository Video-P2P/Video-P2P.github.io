Video-P2P: Video Editing with Cross-attention Control

1The Chinese University of Hong Kong, 2SmartMore 3Adobe

Abstract

This paper presents Video-P2P, a novel framework for real-world video editing with cross-attention control. While attention control has proven effective for image editing with pre-trained generative models, there are currently no large-scale video generation models publicly available. Video-P2P addresses this limitation by demonstrating that a pre-trained image diffusion model can complete various video editing tasks. For video inversion, we propose to tune a Text-to-Set (T2S) model to complete the pivotal inversion and optimize a shared null-text embedding to achieve accurate video inversion with a small memory cost. For attention control, we introduce a novel decoupled-guidance strategy, which uses different guidance strategies for the source and target prompts. The optimized null-text embedding for the source prompt improves reconstruction ability, while an initialized null-text embedding for the target prompt enables editability. Incorporating the attention maps of these two branches enables detailed editing. These technical designs enable various text-driven editing applications, including word swap, prompt refinement, and attention re-weighting. Video-P2P works on real-world videos to generate new characters while optimally maintaining their original poses and scenes. It is also capable of changing global properties such as weather or style.
